Sumary Findings and Incorporation of Findings

From the Competitor Analysis I got the impression that the most successful portfolios were the simpler ones when it comes to esthetics, also, most portfolios are going for black backgrounds, the use of dark backgrounds with light color or white font is dated, and the use of panoramic pictures as a background screams “bootstrap” and since I want to promote myself for my skills at developing, I thought it was important to build it from the ground up.

From the User Interview stage, I find out that is much more important content than form, and everyone I interviewed told me that showing project it is probably the most important and effective way to promote my technical skills.

The Cognitive Walkthrough allowed me to see how potential users interact with the site. I found out the site was very easy to navigate, that users really know what the site is about at first look, and that maybe I went too far in the minimalistic esthetic approach I took. Users also appreciated how responsive the web page was, and how easy adapts to different screens.

The Task Based Online Test allowed me to know how effective was the first 5 seconds of exposure to the site, the conclusion I got is that every user knew immediately what was the purpose of the site, and that the presentation was extremely clean, both goals I set up for myself. The clicker test allowed me to know how self-explanatory and clear where the navigation tags. This goal was achieved too, the heat map was a clear indication that people knew where to find the information about my education, my work experience and the projects I developed in the past.

The most common suggestion I got from the research was that in the esthetic department was a little too simple and lack of color, so I re-do the buttons in the contact and the project section to add a little color and three dimensions to the page. I am currently working on a footer that also have my contact information, since that will frame the content of each section better. I am also adding a little three dimensions to the content section of each page (using shades in the division borders and changing a little the color of the background around that section).

The other suggestion I had from a photographer was that I should change the main picture for one where I am looking directly at the camera, since that inspire confidence to users.

From the midterm, I got remarks that I wasn’t using the HTML5 tags header, section and section tags so I am working on fixing that page by page. My site has 6 html, 7 CSS and 1 JavaScript file, so it is a relatively long fixing in order to not mess up the CSS and JavaScript code when I am changing the target sections.
